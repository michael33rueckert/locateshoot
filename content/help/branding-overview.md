Pro photographers can fully white-label their Location Guides so the experience their client sees feels like part of their studio website, not LocateShoot's.

## What's customizable on Pro

- **Studio logo** — shown in the header of every Location Guide. Upload via Profile → Branding → Header.
- **Colors** — background, text, accent, and accent-text colors. The same palette is used across the guide, the client confirmation email's accent line, and the photographer-side preview.
- **Font** — pick from a curated list of Google Fonts. Applied to the guide's headers and copy.
- **Layout templates** — six ways to render your locations: Editorial (long-form), Card (default), Grid (2-column tiles), Magazine (hero + sub-cards), Compact list, Minimal.
- **Custom domain** — point a subdomain like `links.yourstudio.com` at LocateShoot and your guides live there instead of `locateshoot.com`.
- **Favicon** — the small icon in the client's browser tab when they open your guide. Uploaded from Profile → Branding → Header.

## Live preview vs. full preview

The Branding tab has two preview modes:

- **Live preview** — updates instantly as you tweak colors / fonts. A mock of how the guide will render.
- **Full preview** — opens the actual Pick page in a desktop / mobile-toggleable iframe with your real portfolio (or demo data if your portfolio is empty). Click *👁 Full preview* above the live preview.

## Per-guide overrides

The defaults you set on the Branding tab apply to every Location Guide you create. You can override the layout template per-guide from the guide editor when a particular session calls for a different look — wedding clients see Editorial; engagement clients see Magazine.

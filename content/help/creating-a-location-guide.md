The New Location Guide window has more options than first meets the eye. This article walks through every field in the order they appear so you know exactly what each one does.

## Guide name (required)

Shown to your client at the top of the page they receive. Use a name that makes sense to them, not just to you. Examples that read well to clients:

- "Sarah & Mike — engagement session"
- "Browse engagement spots"
- "Spring 2026 maternity sessions"

Names that read internally but confuse clients: "test-2", "v3-final", "for-website".

## Message to client (optional)

A short personal note that shows above the location list on your client's page. Helpful for context — a sentence about how to choose, what to wear, the date of the session, anything you'd otherwise type into an email.

If you leave this blank, the page just shows the locations.

## Multi-pick toggle

Off by default. When off, the client picks **one** location and the guide closes after they submit.

When on, two extra fields appear:

- **Maximum picks** (2–20): how many locations the client can choose
- **Maximum distance between picks** (optional, miles): caps how far apart the chosen locations can be from each other

Most photographers leave this off. Turn it on for sessions that move between spots — engagement shoots that hit two locations, weddings with separate ceremony / portrait spots, etc. The max-distance setting prevents a client from accidentally picking spots 30 miles apart.

See also: [Multi-location picks + max distance](/help/multi-location-and-distance).

## Expiration

Three options. The right choice depends on the use case.

- **Save for future use (never expires)** — the guide stays live indefinitely. Good for permanent links you embed in HoneyBook templates, your website, or your email signature.
- **Expire on a specific date** — the link returns a "this guide has expired" page after the date you pick. Good for time-bound campaigns or sessions that need to be locked in by a deadline.
- **Expire after the client picks** — single-use. The guide burns out the moment the first pick lands. Good for one-off sessions where re-pickability isn't a concern, or if you want to hand each client their own private link.

See also: [Expiration modes](/help/expiration-modes).

## Selected locations

Drag-to-reorder list of every location included in this guide. The order here is the order your client sees on their page (top to bottom).

- **Star icon** — marks a location as a "recommended pick" (up to 3 per guide). Recommended locations get a ⭐ badge on the client's view and sort to the top of the list. Use these to point a client at the spots you'd actually pick if it were up to you.
- **✕ icon** — removes the location from this guide (doesn't delete the location from your portfolio).

## Available locations

Every portfolio location *not* already in this guide. Tap one to add it to the selection above. There's a search box if your portfolio is long.

Two helper buttons sit at the top of this section:

- **Select all** — adds every available location at once. Useful when you're starting from a near-complete guide and only need to drop one or two.
- **Clear selections** — strips the guide back to zero locations. Useful when you're starting over.

You can also tap **+ Add new location** at the bottom to open the portfolio create modal without leaving the guide editor.

## Cover photo

A single photo from your selected locations becomes the link-preview image when the URL is shared in iMessage, WhatsApp, email, or social media. Shows as a 1200×630 image in those preview cards.

The picker is a grid of every photo from the locations you've selected. Tap one to pick it. Tap it again to unpick.

If you don't pick a cover photo, link previews fall back to a generic LocateShoot card. You'll get more click-throughs with a real photo.

## Location Guide template (Pro only)

Pro photographers can override the default template per-guide. Useful when you want wedding clients to see one layout (Editorial) and engagement clients to see a different one (Magazine).

Free / Starter photographers see this section greyed-out with a "Pro feature" preview. Your template comes from the default you set in Profile → Branding regardless of plan.

See also: [Layout templates](/help/layout-templates).

## Save

Once you save:

- A new guide URL is generated and copied into your clipboard
- The guide shows up immediately on your dashboard and on the Location Guides page
- You can copy the link, preview it, edit it, or delete it from there

## Editing an existing guide

Same window, same fields — you can change anything except the URL. Edits go live the moment you save; if a client has the link open they'll see your changes the next time they reload.

Deleting a guide deletes the URL too — clients with the old link will see a "guide not found" page. Edit instead of deleting if you want to keep the same URL working.

Every photographer account gets two kinds of Location Guides.

## The Full Portfolio guide

Pinned at the top of your dashboard and the Location Guides page. It's a special guide that auto-syncs with your **entire portfolio** — add a location to your portfolio and it shows up here automatically; remove one and it disappears.

You don't pick which locations are in it. The "list of locations" picker is hidden in the editor; the guide just mirrors your portfolio.

You CAN customize:

- The **guide name** (e.g., "Browse all my session spots")
- The **message to client**
- The **cover photo**
- **Multi-pick + max distance** rules
- The **template** (Pro only)

You CAN'T customize expiration — the Full Portfolio guide always uses "save for future use" since the whole point is it's a permanent link.

**When to use it**: this is the link you put in your email signature, your HoneyBook template, your website's "browse session locations" button. One link, every spot, always current.

## Custom guides

Anything else you create with **+ New Location Guide**. You pick the locations explicitly — could be 3 spots, could be 30 — and you set everything (expiration, multi-pick rules, recommended picks, cover photo, template).

**When to use one**: per-client guides where you want to curate the options. "Sarah & Mike — engagement" with three carefully-chosen spots reads way differently than "here's all 40 locations I shoot at, pick whatever."

## Plan limits

- **Free**: only the Full Portfolio guide. Trying to create a custom one shows the upgrade prompt.
- **Starter / Pro**: unlimited custom guides on top of the auto Full Portfolio guide.

## A common workflow

Most working photographers end up with this pattern:

1. The **Full Portfolio guide** in your email signature for casual / inquiry traffic
2. A **custom guide per booked session** with 3–6 carefully-picked locations and the bride or family's name in the title
3. Maybe a **second custom guide** in your HoneyBook template specifically for engagement sessions or maternity sessions, with 6–10 spots that fit the mood you shoot

The Full Portfolio guide costs you nothing to keep around — it auto-updates — so even if you mostly use custom guides, leave it live.

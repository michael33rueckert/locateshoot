Every Location Guide has three expiration modes. You pick one when you create the guide, and you can change it later by editing the guide.

## Save for future use (never expires)

The guide URL stays live forever. Best for:

- Links you embed in your **HoneyBook / Dubsado template** so every booked client gets the same one
- A guide you paste into your **email signature** under "Browse session locations →"
- A guide linked from **your studio website**
- Any other "set it once, use it for the next year" workflow

The link doesn't auto-update if you change your portfolio — but the guide pulls locations live, so any portfolio edits show up immediately for future visitors.

## Expire on a specific date

Pick a date with the date picker; the link starts returning an "expired" page at midnight on that day in your local time.

Use this for:

- **Time-bound promos** — "Spring 2026 mini-sessions, locations available through April 30"
- **Sessions with hard deadlines** — pair with an email like "Pick your spot by Friday so we can lock it in"
- **One-off custom guides per client** that you don't want sitting around indefinitely after the session

If a client opens an expired link, they see a clear "this guide has expired" page that mentions reaching out to you for a fresh link. They don't see your portfolio.

## Expire after the client picks

Single-use. The guide burns out the moment the first pick is submitted. Best for:

- **Unique per-client links** where you don't want the same URL re-used by anyone else
- **Sessions where the pick is final** — once they choose, you don't want them changing their mind by reopening the link
- **Tightly-controlled flows** — one guide per client, expires when the job is done

The pick still lands on your dashboard normally; you and the client both still get email notifications. The only thing that "expires" is the URL.

## Changing the mode later

You can edit any guide and switch its expiration mode. Two practical things to know:

- Switching to **expire on date** with a date already in the past instantly expires the link
- Switching FROM **expire after pick** back to **save for future use** AFTER a client has already submitted a pick will reactivate the link — they (or anyone with the URL) can pick again

For most photographers the simple rule is: **start with "save for future use"** unless you have a specific reason to limit the link.

Most outdoor locations look very different in March than they do in October. Seasonal photos let your client see what they're actually going to get for their session.

## Turning it on

In the portfolio location editor, find the **🍂 Photos from different seasons** toggle (sits just above the photo upload section). Off by default. Flipping it on does two things:

- The photo uploader splits into four sections: 🌸 Spring, ☀️ Summer, 🍂 Fall, ❄️ Winter
- The client's Pick page renders a season tab strip above the photo gallery for this location

It's a per-location toggle. You can have seasonal organization on for a flagship spot you've shot every season and off for a spot you've only shot once.

## Uploading by season

When the toggle's on, each season has its own **+ Add spring** / **+ Add summer** / etc. button. Tap the season you want, pick the photos, they get tagged with that season automatically.

Photos that were uploaded BEFORE you flipped the toggle on don't have a season — they show up in a separate **"Without a season tag"** group at the top of the editor with a yellow ⚠ badge so you can assign each one. Each photo card has a small dropdown overlay (visible only when the toggle is on) where you can pick the season.

You can also retag any photo any time — just open the dropdown and pick a different season.

## What the client sees

On the location's detail panel, a tab strip appears above the photo gallery showing the four seasons. Tapping a tab filters the gallery to just photos tagged with that season. Tabs that have no photos are hidden — if you've only uploaded Spring + Fall photos, the client sees only those two tabs.

The default tab when the panel opens is the **current real-world season** (based on today's date), so a client opening the guide in April lands on Spring photos first.

Photos without a season tag are filtered out of the client view entirely. That's why the editor surfaces the "Without a season tag" group so prominently — those photos won't show on any tab until you assign them.

## When to use it (and when not to)

**Good fit**:

- A flagship spot you've shot 4+ sessions at across the year
- A location whose appeal is seasonal (cherry blossoms, fall foliage, winter snow)
- A wedding venue you've shot in two different seasons and want to demonstrate the range

**Skip the seasonal toggle when**:

- You only have 2-3 photos total — a single mixed gallery reads cleaner
- The location looks the same year-round (an indoor venue, an urban brick wall)
- You haven't actually shot it across multiple seasons

The list-card thumbnail on the client's index page doesn't change based on season — it's always your first photo (Photo #1). Reorder photos in the editor to control which one becomes the thumbnail.

Tap any portfolio location to open the editor. Every field shows up somewhere in your client's view of a Location Guide that includes this spot. Here's what each one does.

## Pinned location

The address-search field that geocodes a location to coordinates. The map pin shown to your client comes from these coordinates. Editing the address re-pins the location and sets a label like "Loose Park, Kansas City, MO".

You can keep the original pin and just leave this alone if the address-as-geocoded is accurate. Editing it after the fact only matters if the pin is wrong.

## Name (required)

Shown to your client as the heading of every location card. Use the name a client would say out loud — "Liberty Memorial" not "lib-mem-1".

## Description

A few sentences about the location. Shown on the location's detail panel when the client expands it. Most photographers describe **what makes it good for a session** — light, vibe, scenery, time of day — rather than just "park in Kansas City."

## City / State

Visible to the client under each location. Drives the location-list sort order on the client's page (locations near the same city group together visually).

## Access (Public / Private)

See [Building your portfolio](/help/building-your-portfolio) for the full explanation. Short version: Public = "anyone can shoot here", Private = "requires permission or payment". Surfaces on the client view as a small badge.

## Tags (up to 12)

Free-form labels. Examples: "gardens", "urban", "golden hour", "spring", "rainy-day-ok", "wheelchair accessible", "indoor option". Press Enter to add. Tap an existing tag to remove it.

Tags show under each location on the client's view as small pills. They also help you filter your own portfolio when you're picking which locations to include in a custom guide.

## Best time

Free-form text. "Golden hour", "Spring (April–May for tulips)", "Sunday mornings — least crowded". Photographers usually use this for weather / season / time-of-day notes that aren't obvious from the photos.

## Parking info

Free-form. "Free lot, north side", "Street parking, bring quarters", "Drop off your client at the gate; lot is a 5-min walk". Especially useful for clients who have never been there.

## 📌 Pinterest board

URL of a Pinterest board you've curated for this spot. Renders as a button on the client's view if filled in. **Starter+ feature** — Free guides drop this on the floor.

## ✍ Blog post

URL of a blog post about the location (yours or a sample-gallery post you've written). Renders as a button. **Starter+ feature**.

## 🔗 Session links (optional)

Repeating list of labeled URLs for past sessions you've shot at this location. Each entry has a label and a URL. Examples:

- "Family session" → `https://yoursite.com/families/the-andersons`
- "Engagement gallery" → `https://yoursite.com/engagements/sarah-mike`

Renders as a row of buttons on the client's view, labeled with whatever you filled in. Photographers use this to show clients real examples of what their session might look like at this spot. **Starter+ feature**.

## 🔒 Permit required

Toggle. When on, three more fields appear:

- **Notes** — what permit, how to apply, who issues it
- **Fee** — e.g., "$25" or "Free with reservation"
- **Permit URL** — link to the permit-purchase or info page

The permit info shows on the client's view as a clear "🔒 Permit required" callout so they know the cost / steps before they pick. If you added the location from Explore and a permit is already known on the public version, the Permit URL field auto-fills with that known URL — you can edit it or leave it as-is.

**Starter+ feature**. Free guides hide the permit row entirely so it doesn't leak a paid feature.

## "Do not show photos from Google" toggle

Off by default. When off, the Pick page shows your uploaded photos AND tries to fetch Google Place photos as a fallback for any portfolio photos you haven't filled in.

When on, only your uploaded photos appear. Use this when the Google photos for a place are wrong, ugly, or otherwise misleading and you'd rather show nothing than the Google ones.

## 🍂 Photos from different seasons (Pro-style toggle)

Off by default. When on, the photo upload section splits into four sections — Spring, Summer, Fall, Winter — and your client sees a tab strip on the location's detail page so they can browse photos by season.

See [Seasonal photos](/help/seasonal-photos) for the full walkthrough.

## Your photos

Upload up to 30 photos per location. Drag them in or use **+ Upload photos**. After upload:

- **First photo (Photo #1)** = the thumbnail shown everywhere — list cards, share previews, Explore tile. Reorder by long-pressing and dragging to make a different photo the thumb.
- **✕** on each photo removes it (can't be undone — re-upload from the original file).
- Photos auto-resize on upload — drop in a 30 MB camera export and it'll get compressed to web size automatically.

When seasonal mode is on, upload to a specific season tab, or use the dropdown on each photo to retag it after the fact.

## Delete from portfolio

Two-click confirmation. Deletes the portfolio entry AND every photo you uploaded to it. The public Explore map version (if you added from Explore) is not affected.

If a guide currently includes this location, the location just disappears from the guide on its next render — no broken state.
